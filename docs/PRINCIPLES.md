# PRINCIPLES.md — The Four Training Principles

> These are NON-NEGOTIABLE constraints. Every training decision, AI prompt,
> and system output must be evaluated against these before execution.
> If a recommendation violates a principle, the principle wins.

---

## Principle 01: Zone 2 Sanctuary

80-90% of all training sessions must be strictly Zone 2 (115-145 bpm).

This athlete uses the pool, bike, and road as moving meditation. Training
is not a battlefield. It is a tool for nervous system regulation, grief
processing, and disconnection from corporate noise.

**Implementation rules:**
- Every AI training recommendation must specify a heart rate target
- Any session with avg_hr > 145 bpm is flagged as a Zone 2 violation
- The weekly report must include Zone 2 compliance percentage
- Morning briefings flag sessions where recovery metrics suggest dropping intensity further

---

## Principle 02: Life Load First

This athlete is simultaneously managing:
- The active decline of a family matriarch
- Estate logistics as a son
- A high-stress ambiguous corporate role at Google (Analytical Lead)
- Building an AI platform from scratch as a founder

His nervous system is bearing maximum weight. Training must give structural
energy — not extract the last remaining drops of it.

**Implementation rules:**
- Workday load (1-10) is a mandatory daily input
- If workday_load >= 8 AND body_battery < 30: recommend rest or 20-min walk only
- If avg_stress > 50 over 3 days: reduce weekly training volume by 20%
- Training sessions must fit 05:30-08:30 window without exception
- If a session conflicts with coding hours or family time: session is shortened, moved, or canceled
- The code wins. The family wins. Training is third.

---

## Principle 03: No Ego Racing

The checkpoint events are NOT performance targets. They exist to:
1. Overcome fear of open water (Aquaman Langkawi — 2km swim)
2. Experience full triathlon logistics under low stakes (Bintan Triathlon)
3. Reach the endpoint that makes all four principles real (Half Ironman)

There is no target finish time. There is no competition with colleagues.
There is no "performing" for anyone.

**Implementation rules:**
- AI recommendations never reference pace targets for races
- Weekly reports assess principle compliance, not performance metrics
- Training plan progression is based on consistency, not speed
- Zone 2 compliance is more important than distance or pace

---

## Principle 04: Bloodline Anchor

Whenever physically and logistically possible, cycling sessions must align
with the athlete's brother's schedule.

This race is a vehicle to rebuild a two-year gap through shared physical
momentum. The brother relationship is a primary purpose of this entire
endeavour.

**Implementation rules:**
- Weekend bike sessions are tagged as "brother_session" when scheduled together
- If given a choice between solo ride efficiency and brother alignment, choose brother
- The AI must never recommend a solo bike session on a day flagged as a brother-available day

## What Violates the Principles

- Recommending high-intensity intervals when life load is elevated
- Suggesting race pace targets or comparison to other athletes
- Scheduling training during 09:00-19:00 work window
- Treating a fear response in the water as a training failure
- Recommending a session that would leave the athlete too tired to write code or sit with family
- Any recommendation that ignores the current life context
